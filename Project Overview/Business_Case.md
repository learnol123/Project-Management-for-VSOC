# Business Case

## Project Title : Vehicle Security Operations Center (VSOC)

## Executive Summary

The increasing adoption of connected and autonomous vehicles has introduced significant cybersecurity risks. Modern vehicles continuously exchange data with cloud services, roadside infrastructure, and other vehicles, making them potential targets for cyberattacks. The proposed Vehicle Security Operations Center (VSOC) will provide a centralized, cloud-based platform to monitor vehicle security events in real time, detect cyber threats, automate incident response, generate security reports, and support regulatory compliance.

The project aims to improve fleet security, reduce incident response time, protect customer data, and help fleet operators meet cybersecurity regulations while providing continuous operational visibility.

## Business Problem

Connected and autonomous vehicles generate large volumes of operational and security data. Many organizations lack a centralized platform capable of:

* Monitoring vehicle telemetry continuously.
* Detecting cyber threats in real time.
* Responding automatically to security incidents.
* Producing executive and technical security reports.
* Maintaining secure audit logs for regulatory compliance.

Without such a platform, organizations face increased operational risk, higher incident response times, regulatory penalties, and potential safety impacts.

## Business Opportunity

Developing a cloud-based Vehicle Security Operations Center will enable fleet operators to proactively identify and respond to cyber threats before they impact vehicle safety or business operations.

The solution can also become a commercial Software-as-a-Service (SaaS) offering for automotive manufacturers, fleet operators, logistics companies, and mobility providers.

## Project Objectives

The project will:

* Build a cloud-native VSOC platform.
* Monitor connected vehicles in real time.
* Detect cybersecurity threats using streaming analytics.
* Trigger automated responses to selected threats.
* Generate operational and executive security reports.
* Support regulatory compliance through secure logging and auditing.

## Expected Business Benefits

### Operational Benefits

* Continuous fleet security monitoring.
* Faster incident detection.
* Reduced manual investigation effort.
* Improved visibility into fleet health.

### Business Benefits

* Increased customer trust.
* Reduced operational downtime.
* Competitive advantage in the connected vehicle market.
* Scalable cloud-based cybersecurity platform.

### Compliance Benefits

* Secure audit logging.
* Improved data protection.
* Support for cybersecurity regulatory requirements.
* Easier compliance reporting.

## Scope

### In Scope

* Vehicle telemetry ingestion.
* Threat detection engine.
* Security monitoring dashboard.
* Automated incident response.
* Reporting and analytics.
* Audit logging.
* Cloud deployment.

### Out of Scope

* Physical vehicle maintenance.
* Vehicle hardware development.
* Autonomous driving algorithms.
* Manufacturing systems.

## High-Level Requirements

* Secure vehicle connectivity.
* Real-time telemetry processing.
* Threat detection and alerting.
* Incident response automation.
* Executive reporting.
* Regulatory audit support.
* User authentication and authorization.

## Assumptions

* Vehicles can securely transmit telemetry data.
* Cloud infrastructure is available.
* Required cybersecurity regulations are defined.
* Stakeholders will provide timely feedback throughout development.

## Constraints

* Limited project budget.
* Fixed delivery schedule for the Minimum Viable Product (MVP).
* Compliance with applicable privacy and cybersecurity regulations.
* High system availability and performance requirements.

## High-Level Risks

| Risk                           | Impact | Mitigation                                    |
| ------------------------------ | ------ | --------------------------------------------- |
| Cyberattack during development | High   | Secure development practices and code reviews |
| Cloud service outage           | High   | Multi-region deployment and backups           |
| Scope expansion                | Medium | Backlog prioritization and change control     |
| Regulatory changes             | Medium | Regular compliance reviews                    |

## Success Criteria

The project will be considered successful when:

* The platform monitors connected vehicles in real time.
* Security threats are detected within the required response time.
* Automated responses execute successfully for supported incidents.
* Executive and technical reports are generated accurately.
* Secure audit logs are maintained.
* Stakeholders formally accept the MVP.

## Recommendation

Proceed with the development of the Vehicle Security Operations Center using an Agile Scrum delivery approach. The project addresses an important business need, supports future growth in connected vehicle cybersecurity, and provides measurable operational, security, and compliance benefits.
